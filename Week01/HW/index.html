Dubai Chewy Cookie

Description

This Dubai Chewy Cookie is one of the most viral desserts in Korea right now, with people waiting six hours in line before opening just to try it. It features a soft, stretchy marshmallow-based chocolate exterior and a rich pistachio kataifi filling thatâ€™s nutty and crunchy. At first glance, it almost looks like mochi, but the texture actually comes from marshmallow, not rice flour. No baking required, incredibly indulgent, and perfect for gifting or special treats.

Ingredients

  • 150 g kataifi (finely chopped)
  • 20 g butter (unsalted)
  • 250 g pistachio cream
  • 200 g marshmallow
  • 30 g butter
  • 15 g cocoa powder
  • 20 g milk powder
  • extra cocoa powder (for rolling/dusting)

Instructions

  1. Finely cut the kataifi strands.
  2. Melt the butter in a pan over medium heat. Add kataifi and toast, stirring constantly, until evenly golden brown.
  3. Transfer to a bowl and mix with pistachio cream until fully combined.
  4. Place the mixture in the freezer for 15 minutes to firm up.
  5. Divide into 10 equal portions and roll into balls. Place on a tray and refrigerate while preparing the exterior.
  6. Sift the cocoa powder and milk powder together; set aside.
  7. In a nonstick skillet over low heat, melt the butter. Add marshmallows and gently stir until about 90% melted.
  8. Turn off the heat and immediately add the sifted cocoa and milk powder. Mix until smooth and fully combined.
  9. Transfer to parchment paper or a nonstick mat and let cool completely.
  10. Lightly oil your hands. Divide the cooled marshmallow mixture into 10 equal pieces.
  11. Flatten one piece, place a filling ball in the center, and wrap to seal. Roll gently into a smooth ball.
  12. Dust generously with cocoa powder.
